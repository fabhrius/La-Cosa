No mind can carry the whole shape of the world.

To perceive
is to carve away.

Every vision is a fragment.
Every truth,
a reflection upon dark water.

What you name “reality”
is only the form your spirit is able to contain.

Thus,
all beings walk within invisible boundaries,
seeing,
and not seeing.

— Fabhrius

(From “Omniscientis”, the lost codex of Lluviara)

Elara once believed the world was exactly as she perceived it.

Clear.
Solid.
Certain.

But every village told a different story.
Every traveler described a different truth.

And slowly,
she understood something unsettling:

People do not see reality itself.

They see the shape their minds are able to hold.

Fear changes it.
Hope changes it.
Pain changes it.

Even memory reshapes the world.

From that day forward,
Elara no longer searched for eyes that never failed.

She searched for eyes willing to see differently.


-----------------------------------



Ella creía que el mundo era exactamente como lo percibía.
Claro.
Sólido.
Cierto.

Pero cada pueblo contaba una historia diferente.
Cada viajero describía una verdad distinta.

Las personas no ven la realidad en sí misma.
Ven la forma que sus mentes son capaces de captar.

El miedo la transforma.
La esperanza la transforma.
El dolor la transforma.

La memoria remodela el mundo.

Contra esto, solo hay una cosa que puedes hacer...

Busca con ojos dispuestos a ver de otra manera.
