Why is reality so complicated?

Every answer leads to another question.

Every discovery reveals another mystery.

Every map uncovers new territory.

And yet...

the universe somehow fits inside itself.

So why can't it fit inside our minds?

------------------------

To understand reality,

we simplify it.

We create models.

Concepts.

Categories.

Stories.

And they work.

But if understanding requires removing complexity...

how do we know what we removed wasn't the answer?


--------------------------------


The universe may be too complex to understand completely.

But there is something stranger.

A universe complex enough

to create minds

that notice it is too complex to understand.

How much complexity is required

to discover complexity?


--------------------------------

The more we learn,

the larger reality becomes.

The more we discover,

the more there is to discover.

The more we understand,

the more we realize we do not understand.

So tell me...

if knowledge keeps increasing ignorance,

how will we know when we are finally wise?


----------------------
