
Las palabras de Fabhrius la estremecieron.

Parecían estar hablándole directamente a ella, atravesando la distancia de los siglos.

Volvió a observar la sala.

Los libros.
Las paredes.
La luz temblando sobre la piedra.

Todo parecía igual.

Y sin embargo...

algo había cambiado.

La realidad siempre había estado allí.

Delante de sus ojos.

Pero ver no es lo mismo que comprender.

Y si toda visión es un fragmento,
es posible que cada persona vea un mundo diferente.

Así, no será posible alcanzar el conocimiento
sin aprender primero a cuestionar nuestras propias certezas.

Porque tal vez una parte importante del misterio no esté en el mundo.

Sino dentro de quien observa.

The words of Fabhrius shook her.

It felt as though he were speaking directly to her across the distance of centuries.

She looked around the chamber once more.

The books.

The walls.

The torchlight trembling upon the stone.

Everything seemed the same.

And yet...

something had changed.

Reality had always been there.

Before her eyes.

But seeing is not the same as understanding.

And if every vision is a fragment,

then perhaps each person sees a different world.

Thus, knowledge cannot be attained

without first learning to question our own certainties.

Because perhaps an important part of the mystery is not in the world.

But within the one who observes.

So each creates a simpler version.

A representation.

And every representation leaves something out.

If every vision is a fragment,

then no conclusion can ever be final.

And perhaps each of us beholds a different world.

The path to knowledge begins when we learn to question our own certainties.

Because perhaps,

an important part of the mystery is not in the world.

But within the one who observes.
